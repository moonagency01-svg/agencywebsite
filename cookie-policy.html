Cookie Policy

Last updated: July 13, 2026

This page explains what cookies this site uses, why, and how you can control them.

What is a cookie?

A cookie is a small piece of data stored in your browser, usually to remember a preference or setting between visits.

Cookies we use

Cookie Set by Purpose Duration
cookie_consent This site (essential) Remembers whether you accepted or rejected non-essential cookies, so we don't ask on every page 12 months
_ga, _ga_* Google Analytics Distinguishes visitors and sessions for traffic statistics — only set if you accept non-essential cookies Up to 2 years
YouTube embed cookies Google / YouTube Set when you play an embedded tutorial video; used by YouTube for playback and, depending on your settings with Google, personalization Set by Google — varies

Google Analytics only loads at all if you click "Accept all" on our cookie banner. If you click "Reject non-essential" (or never respond), no analytics cookies are set and no traffic data is sent to Google.

Your choices

When you first visit, a banner lets you accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can change your choice anytime using the "Manage Cookie Preferences" link in the footer of any page.

You can also control or delete cookies directly through your browser settings — every major browser has a way to view, block, or clear cookies, usually under Settings → Privacy.

Third-party cookies

Embedded YouTube videos on our Tutorial page are hosted by Google. If you interact with a video player, YouTube may set its own cookies according to Google's Cookie Policy, which we don't control.

If you accept non-essential cookies, we also use Google Analytics to understand overall site traffic (which pages are visited, roughly where from, and general device type). This never happens unless you accept — see Google's Cookie Policy for how they handle this data.

Changes to this policy

If the cookies this site uses change — for example, if we add analytics in the future — we'll update this page and the "Last updated" date above.
